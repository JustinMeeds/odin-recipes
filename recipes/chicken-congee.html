Chicken Congee Recipe

Description

Chicken congee is a comforting rice porridge that's a staple in many Asian cuisines. It's made by simmering rice in a flavorful broth until it breaks down into a creamy consistency. This recipe uses chicken thighs for added protein and flavor, along with ginger for warmth and a subtle spice. It’s perfect for cold days or when you need a nourishing, easy-to-digest meal.

This congee is versatile and can be topped with green onions, soy sauce, sesame oil, or even a boiled egg for added richness. It’s a dish that warms the soul and is loved for its simplicity and heartiness.

Ingredients

  • Chicken thighs: 300g
  • Chicken stock (8 cups)
  • Ginger (1-2 inches, sliced)
  • Salt (To taste)
  • Seasame Oil (1 Tbsp)
  • Optional toppings:
    • Green onions, chopped
    • Soy sauce
    • Sesame oil

Steps

  1. In a large pot, rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear.
  2. Add the chicken stock, water, and sliced ginger to the pot. Bring to a boil.
  3. Lower the heat to a simmer and add the chicken thighs. Cover and let it cook for 1.5 hours, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
  4. Once the rice has broken down into a creamy consistency, remove the chicken thighs, shred them with a fork, and return the meat to the pot.
  5. Season the congee with salt to taste.
  6. Add seasame oil and mix.
  7. Serve hot with optional toppings such as green onions, soy sauce, or sesame oil.
